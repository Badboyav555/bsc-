# Botany — Unit 1: Cell Biology

## Overview
The **cell** is the fundamental structural and functional unit of all living organisms. Cell biology studies cell structure, function, and behaviour.

---

## 1. Cell Theory

Proposed by **Schleiden and Schwann** (1839), refined by **Virchow** (1855):
1. All living organisms are made of cells
2. Cell is the basic unit of life
3. All cells arise from pre-existing cells

---

## 2. Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Cells

| Feature | Prokaryotic | Eukaryotic |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Nucleus | Absent (nucleoid) | True nucleus present |
| DNA | Circular, naked | Linear, with histones |
| Size | 1–10 µm | 10–100 µm |
| Organelles | Absent | Present |
| Examples | Bacteria | Plants, Animals, Fungi |

---

## 3. Plant Cell Organelles

| Organelle | Function |
|----------|---------|
| Cell wall (cellulose) | Rigidity and support |
| Chloroplast | Photosynthesis |
| Mitochondria | Cellular respiration |
| Vacuole | Storage, turgor pressure |
| Ribosome | Protein synthesis |
| Golgi body | Secretion and packaging |
| Nucleus | Hereditary material (DNA) |
| ER (rough) | Protein synthesis + transport |

---

## 4. Cell Division

### Mitosis
- Produces **2 identical diploid** daughter cells
- For growth and repair
- Phases: Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase

### Meiosis
- Produces **4 haploid** daughter cells (gametes)
- Two divisions: Meiosis I (reductional) + Meiosis II (equational)
- Causes genetic variation through crossing over

| Feature | Mitosis | Meiosis |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Daughter cells | 2 | 4 |
| Chromosome no. | Unchanged (2n) | Halved (n) |
| Crossing over | Absent | Present |
| Purpose | Growth, repair | Sexual reproduction |

---

## 5. Osmosis and Water Relations

```
Water potential = Osmotic potential + Pressure potential
```

- **Osmosis:** Water moves from high to low water potential
- **Turgid cell:** Fully inflated with water
- **Plasmolysis:** Cytoplasm shrinks in hypertonic solution
- **Flaccid cell:** No pressure potential

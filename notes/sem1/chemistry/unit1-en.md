# Physics - Unit 1: Mechanics & Motion

## Overview

Mechanics is the branch of physics that deals with the motion of objects and the forces that cause or change that motion. This unit covers the fundamental concepts of classical mechanics including kinematics, Newton's laws, and their applications.

---

## 1. Kinematics

Kinematics is the study of motion **without considering its causes**.

### Key Quantities

| Quantity | Symbol | Unit | Description |
|---------|--------|------|-------------|
| Displacement | s or x | metre (m) | Change in position |
| Velocity | v | m/s | Rate of change of displacement |
| Acceleration | a | m/s² | Rate of change of velocity |
| Time | t | second (s) | Duration |

### Equations of Motion (for uniform acceleration)

The three equations of motion for a body moving with uniform acceleration `a`, initial velocity `u`, final velocity `v`, and displacement `s` over time `t`:

```
v = u + at                   ... (1st equation)
s = ut + ½at²               ... (2nd equation)
v² = u² + 2as               ... (3rd equation)
```

> **Remember:** These equations only apply when acceleration is **constant** (uniform).

### Types of Motion

1. **Uniform Motion** — equal displacement in equal intervals of time
2. **Non-Uniform Motion** — unequal displacement in equal intervals
3. **Projectile Motion** — motion under gravity at an angle
4. **Circular Motion** — motion along a circular path

## 2. Newton's Laws of Motion

### First Law (Law of Inertia)

> *"A body at rest remains at rest, and a body in uniform motion continues in uniform motion in a straight line, unless acted upon by an external force."*

- This defines **inertia**: the tendency of an object to resist any change in its state.
- **Mass** is the measure of inertia.

### Second Law

> *"The rate of change of momentum of a body is directly proportional to the applied force, and takes place in the direction of that force."*

```
F = ma
```

Where:
- F = Net force (Newton, N)
- m = Mass (kg)
- a = Acceleration (m/s²)

**1 Newton** = force that gives 1 kg mass an acceleration of 1 m/s².

### Third Law (Action-Reaction)

> *"For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction."*

**Examples:**
- Rocket propulsion: gases expelled backward, rocket moves forward
- Walking: foot pushes ground backward, ground pushes foot forward
- Recoil of a gun when fired

## 3. Momentum and Impulse

### Linear Momentum

```
p = mv
```

- **p** = momentum (kg·m/s)
- **m** = mass (kg)
- **v** = velocity (m/s)

Momentum is a **vector quantity** — it has both magnitude and direction.

### Law of Conservation of Momentum

> *"In the absence of external forces, the total momentum of a system remains constant."*

```
m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
```

### Impulse

Impulse = Change in momentum

```
J = F × t = Δp = m(v - u)
```

## 4. Work, Energy, and Power

### Work

```
W = F · s · cos θ
```

- **W** = Work done (Joule, J)
- **F** = Force applied (N)
- **s** = Displacement (m)
- **θ** = Angle between force and displacement

Work is **zero** when:
- Force is zero
- Displacement is zero
- Force ⊥ Displacement (θ = 90°)

### Kinetic Energy

```
KE = ½mv²
```

### Potential Energy (Gravitational)

```
PE = mgh
```

### Work-Energy Theorem

```
Net Work = Change in Kinetic Energy
W_net = ΔKE = ½mv² - ½mu²
```

### Power

```
P = W/t = F × v
```

- Unit: **Watt (W)** = 1 Joule/second

---

## 5. Friction

Friction is a force that **opposes relative motion** between surfaces in contact.

### Types

| Type | Description | Formula |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Static friction | Prevents start of motion | f_s ≤ μ_s × N |
| Kinetic friction | Opposes sliding motion | f_k = μ_k × N |
| Rolling friction | Opposes rolling | Smallest of all types |

Where:
- **μ** = coefficient of friction (dimensionless)
- **N** = Normal force

> **Note:** μ_s > μ_k always (it's harder to start motion than to maintain it).

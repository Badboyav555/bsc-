# Zoology — Unit 1: Animal Kingdom Classification

## Overview
Animal kingdom classification groups animals based on **body organisation, symmetry, coelom type, and reproductive patterns**.

---

## 1. Basis of Classification

| Criterion | Types |
|-----------|-------|
| Body symmetry | Asymmetrical, Radial, Bilateral |
| Body cavity | Acoelomate, Pseudocoelomate, Coelomate |
| Germinal layers | Diploblastic (2 layers), Triploblastic (3 layers) |
| Development | Protostomes, Deuterostomes |
| Notochord | Chordates, Non-chordates |

---

## 2. Major Phyla

### Phylum Porifera (Sponges)
- Simplest multicellular animals
- Body full of pores (ostia) and canals
- Skeleton of spicules (silica or CaCO₃)
- Examples: Sycon, Spongilla, Euspongia

### Phylum Cnidaria (Coelenterata)
- Radially symmetrical, diploblastic
- Cnidoblasts (stinging cells) for defence and prey capture
- Polymorphism: polyp form (sedentary) and medusa form (free-swimming)
- Examples: Hydra, Aurelia (jellyfish), Coral, Sea anemone

### Phylum Platyhelminthes (Flatworms)
- First bilaterally symmetrical animals
- Triploblastic, acoelomate
- Many are parasitic
- Examples: Planaria (free-living), Fasciola (liver fluke), Taenia (tapeworm)

### Phylum Nematoda (Roundworms)
- Pseudocoelomate, unsegmented
- Cuticle (tough outer layer)
- Examples: Ascaris, Wuchereria (filariasis), Ancylostoma (hookworm)

### Phylum Annelida (Segmented worms)
- True coelom, segmented body
- Closed circulatory system
- Examples: Nereis (earthworm), Hirudinaria (leech), Lumbricus

### Phylum Arthropoda (Largest phylum)
- Jointed appendages, exoskeleton of chitin
- Open circulatory system
- Examples: Prawn, Cockroach, Butterfly, Spider, Scorpion

### Phylum Mollusca
- Soft body with mantle; most have shell
- Examples: Pila (apple snail), Octopus, Sepia, Unio (freshwater mussel)

### Phylum Echinodermata
- Spiny skin, radially symmetrical (adults)
- Water vascular system for locomotion
- Examples: Asterias (starfish), Echinus (sea urchin), Holothuria (sea cucumber)

### Phylum Chordata
- Notochord, dorsal nerve cord, pharyngeal gill slits

| Sub-phylum | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Urochordata | Ascidia |
| Cephalochordata | Amphioxus |
| Vertebrata | Fish, Frog, Lizard, Pigeon, Rabbit |

## 3. Vertebrate Classes

| Class | Features | Examples |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Pisces (Fish) | Scales, gills, cold-blooded | Rohu, Scoliodon |
| Amphibia | Dual life, moist skin | Frog, Salamander |
| Reptilia | Scales, 3-chambered heart | Lizard, Snake, Turtle |
| Aves (Birds) | Feathers, wings, warm-blooded | Pigeon, Crow |
| Mammalia | Hair, mammary glands, warm-blooded | Rat, Bat, Whale, Human |
